Predicting Arrests — Regression Model
A multiple regression model built on 1996 crime data from 2,197 communities to predict arrest rates. Five variables — population, density, income, race composition, and youth population — collectively explained 65% of arrest variation, with population as the strongest predictor.

About me

Strategy meets
creativity.

I hold a Bachelor of Science in Integrated Marketing Communications from John Brown University, with a passion for research and how data can inform smarter, more meaningful strategy. My academic work spans integrated marketing plans, consumer research, data visualization, and statistical modeling — across industries, formats, and scales.

Outside of marketing, I write short stories and poems. Storytelling in every form has always been where I feel most at home. When I'm not working, you'll find me deep in a romance novel, watching history documentaries (Egyptian history especially — I will absolutely geek out about it), or wandering through an art museum longer than anyone expected.

If you like my work, please reach out. I'd love to talk.
